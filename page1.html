Before the Music starts

Yes, it might sound cliché,  but I’ve always been fascinated by the quiet moments we rarely talk about - waiting for your friend in a cozy café, wandering through an unfamiliar street, the first drop of rain before a storm. Those fleeting pauses hold a special kind of magic.

My music comes from those subtle, hard-to-name emotions. I love when something stirs inside you, but you’re not sure why.

As you listen, don’t worry about what it “means.” Let it take you where it will. Be present and just live a little.

A little story

It all began with an old piano in my parents’ house. At five years old, a loud, mysterious instrument like that was irresistible. I went on to complete twelve years of classical piano training at the Musikakademie Eupen under the wonderful guidance of Henrike Jardon, while Mr. Reul opened the door to improvisation and jazz.

I started out improvising with simple four-chord patterns, slowly layering in new ideas. Much of my growth has come from watching and learning from artists I admire - for me, that’s Joep Beving, Hania Rani, Ólafur Arnalds and many others...

So far, I’ve released two albums. My first release was Shapes of Emotions (2020), recorded on a grand stage piano, that explores the rhythms and dynamics of nature. My newest album, La Visite du Manoir (2024), was recorded on the upright piano (Herbert & Leutke) I first played as a child. It captures the eerie, dreamy feeling of wandering through abandoned houses and forgotten manors — and features my brother Jérome on violin, adding a beautiful layer to the sound.

This space is where I try to understand what my music wants to become and share it freely. I like to imagine it playing somewhere out there - in a living room, on a small sound system, or even on another piano. You can download my sheet music for free here.

Thank you so much for listening and supporting my work. Enjoy :)
